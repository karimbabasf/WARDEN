WARDEN — The Agent That Watches Your Agents
A non-technical overview
===========================================


THE ONE-LINER
-------------
WARDEN is a Mac app that quietly supervises the AI coding assistants you
already use — and tells you, with receipts, when they're going off the rails.


THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES
---------------------
More and more real coding is being done by AI agents — tools like Claude Code
and Codex that don't just autocomplete, but actually read your project, make
changes, and run commands on their own. They're powerful, but they're also a
black box. They go in circles, develop bad habits, quietly burn money, and make
confident mistakes. When something goes wrong, you're left scrolling through an
enormous log trying to figure out what happened and why.

And it's getting harder, not easier: people are starting to run several agents
at once. There's no control tower. You're managing a team of tireless workers
you can't actually see.


WHAT WARDEN DOES
----------------
WARDEN runs in the background on your Mac, always on. It reads the work logs
your AI agents leave behind — the complete record of everything they did — and
continuously analyzes them for trouble: wasted effort, risky shortcuts,
repeated mistakes, ballooning cost.

When you want to know what's going on, you hit one keyboard shortcut and a
"war room" overlay appears: a cinematic command-center view that gives you a
clear diagnosis. Here's where your agent went wrong. Here's exactly why. Here's
how to fix it.

The thing that makes it trustworthy is that every conclusion comes with
evidence. WARDEN doesn't just say "your agent was inefficient" — it points you
to the exact moment in the transcript it's talking about, the specific quote,
so you can verify it yourself. It's a diagnosis with citations, not a vague
vibe.


WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT
-----------------------
- It's honest. The dramatic visuals aren't decoration — every glowing node and
  pulse maps to something real that actually happened. Nothing is faked for
  effect.

- It's private and safe. Everything happens locally on your machine. Today it
  only watches and advises — it never touches or changes your actual code.

- It speaks human. The whole point is to turn a wall of impenetrable logs into
  a clear story a person can act on.


WHERE IT'S HEADED — THE END GOAL
--------------------------------
WARDEN started as a kind of forensic investigator: something that looks back at
what an agent did and explains what went wrong. The vision is to grow it into a
live control tower for your AI workforce.

The path there, in plain terms:

- See them live. Instead of looking back at finished work, watch every agent and
  sub-agent that's running right now, as living shapes on a radar — which ones
  are busy, which are stuck, how much "headroom" each has left.
  (This is the part being built now.)

- Let it act. Move from "here's the fix you should make" to "want me to make
  it?" — so WARDEN can actually step in and correct an agent, not just point at
  the problem.

- Make it ambient. Add voice, so it can warn you out loud, and support every
  kind of AI agent, not just a couple.

The end state: as AI agents take over more and more of the actual coding, and
as you go from running one to running many, WARDEN becomes the single place
where a human stays in control — seeing the whole fleet at a glance, catching
agents the moment they go wrong, understanding why in plain language, and
stepping in to fix it. It's the oversight, quality, and trust layer for a world
where you manage AI workers instead of writing every line yourself.
